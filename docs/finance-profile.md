# Finance Profile

Persistent planning assumptions for the Personal Finance Assembly Line.

These assumptions are reviewed and confirmed (or overridden) during Phase 3 of every monthly review.

> **Note:** All values below are examples. Replace with your actual figures before using this system.

Fixed percentages should be avoided where possible.

Where contributions are based on household income, the methodology should be recorded rather than a fixed percentage.

Percentages are derived during the monthly review from current income figures.

## Cash Buffers

| Assumption             | Value                    | Notes                                                               |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Minimum cash buffer    | £500 per household       | Absolute floor for available cash after all planned allocations     |
| Preferred cash buffer  | £1,000                   | Target month-end cash position before discretionary allocations     |
| Buffer methodology     | Preserve liquidity first | Cashflow stability takes precedence over accelerated debt reduction |
| Emergency fund target  | £20,000 - £22,000        | Long-term target                                                    |
| Emergency fund current | (update monthly)       | Updated during reconcile phase                                      |

### Minimum Cash Buffer

The minimum buffer is the absolute floor.

If a proposed plan would reduce available cash below this amount, the plan must be revised unless an explicit override is approved.

Examples of acceptable overrides:

* Vehicle replacement
* Major household emergency
* Temporary income disruption

### Preferred Cash Buffer

The preferred buffer is the normal planning target.

Monthly plans should aim to preserve this balance before:

* Accelerated debt repayments
* Additional BT overpayments
* Discretionary investments

### Buffer Methodology

When competing priorities exist:

1. Preserve minimum buffer.
2. Meet essential obligations.
3. Maintain agreed BT targets.
4. Maintain preferred buffer.
5. Contribute to emergency fund.
6. Consider accelerated repayments and optimisation activities.

### Monthly Review Rules

During each monthly review:

1. Update current cash position.
2. Verify buffer levels remain appropriate.
3. Document any temporary buffer override.
4. Reassess preferred buffer following major life or financial changes.

## Mortgage

| Assumption           | Value                                | Notes                                                                     |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Split methodology    | Proportional to net household income | Recalculated each monthly review using current net monthly income figures |
| Overpayment target   | £0                                   | Set if making regular overpayments                                        |
| Fallback methodology | Manual override                      | Used only if proportional allocation is temporarily unsuitable            |

### Mortgage Contribution Methodology

Mortgage contributions are calculated based on each person's share of total net household income for the planning month.

Example:

If Romeo net income = £5,118.56 and Kelly net income = £2,766.10:

* Romeo contribution = 64.9%
* Kelly contribution = 35.1%

The percentages are not fixed and must be recalculated whenever income changes.

### Monthly Review Rules

During each monthly review:

1. Update the current mortgage payment amount.
2. Update both net income figures.
3. Recalculate contribution percentages.
4. Calculate each person's mortgage contribution.
5. Document any temporary override.

### Override Rule

If proportional allocation is temporarily unsuitable due to:

* employment changes
* income disruption
* large one-off expenses
* cashflow preservation requirements

a temporary override may be applied and documented in the monthly review.

### Balance Transfer Repayment Methodology

The objective is to clear all balance transfer balances before their promotional periods expire while maintaining sufficient household liquidity.

For each balance transfer card:

1. Calculate the required monthly payment to clear the balance before the promotional expiry date.
2. Compare the required payment against the current operational target.
3. Flag any variance between the two.
4. Review and adjust targets during the monthly planning cycle if necessary.

### Priority Rules

1. Essential household obligations take priority:

   * Mortgage
   * Household bills
   * Insurance
   * Other committed expenses

2. Maintain agreed balance transfer operational targets wherever cashflow allows.

3. Direct debits count towards operational targets.

4. Only the difference between the direct debit and operational target should be manually topped up.

5. Accelerated balance transfer repayments should only be made if:

   * Cashflow remains healthy
   * Minimum cash buffer is maintained
   * Emergency fund objectives are not compromised

### Monthly Review Checks

During each monthly review:

* Update current balances.
* Verify promotional expiry dates from statements.
* Verify direct debit amounts.
* Recalculate required monthly repayment.
* Compare required repayment versus operational target.
* Document any shortfall or acceleration decision.

## Planning Rule Hierarchy

When there is a conflict between allocations, apply this priority order:

### Tier 1 - Non-Negotiable

1. Mortgage payment
2. Essential household bills
3. Minimum cash buffer
4. Minimum credit card payments


### Tier 2 - Agreed Financial Commitments

5. Balance Transfer operational targets (MBNA and M&S)
6. Required vehicle, insurance and other committed household obligations
7. Tithe
8. Amex repayment in full where cashflow permits


### Tier 3 - Financial Resilience

9. Preferred cash buffer maintenance
10. Emergency fund contribution

### Tier 4 - Financial Optimisation

11. Accelerated balance transfer repayments
12. Additional debt overpayments
13. Investment contributions beyond pension deductions

### Exception Rule

The monthly planning phase may temporarily override lower-priority items where required to:

* Preserve household liquidity
* Avoid emergency fund depletion
* Manage temporary income disruption
* Accommodate major planned expenditures (e.g. vehicle replacement)

Any override must be documented in the monthly plan.

## Override Policy

FinanceOS distinguishes between:

1. Temporary overrides
2. Permanent policy changes

### Temporary Overrides

A temporary override is a one-off deviation from the Finance Profile due to current circumstances.

Examples:

* Pausing Emergency Fund contributions
* Reducing a Balance Transfer target
* Increasing the minimum cash buffer
* Deferring accelerated debt repayments
* Preserving liquidity for a vehicle purchase

Temporary overrides should be documented in the relevant monthly review artefact (typically Assumptions, Position or Plan).

Format:

```text
Override: [assumption or policy]
Reason: [why the override is required]
Normal Value: [value from finance-profile.md]
Override Value: [value used this month]
Review Date: [month when override should be reassessed]
```

### Permanent Policy Changes

A permanent policy change reflects a deliberate update to how FinanceOS operates.

Examples:

* Changing the Emergency Fund target
* Changing the mortgage split methodology
* Changing Balance Transfer repayment methodology
* Changing the Planning Rule Hierarchy

Permanent changes should be made directly to:

```text
docs/finance-profile.md
```

and committed with a descriptive commit message explaining the rationale.

### Override Review Rule

At the start of each monthly review:

1. Review all active overrides.
2. Determine whether each override should:

   * be removed,
   * remain active,
   * become a permanent policy change.
3. Document the decision within the monthly review artefacts.

## Major Planned Expenditure Policy

Examples:
- Vehicle replacement
- Home maintenance
- Major travel
- Medical expenses

Methodology:

Where a known large expenditure is expected within the next 6 months:

1. Preserve liquidity.
2. Delay accelerated debt repayments if required.
3. Reduce Emergency Fund contributions if required.
4. Document temporary overrides.
5. Revert to standard priorities once expenditure is complete.

## Tithe

| Assumption        | Value                    | Notes                                                  |
| ----------------- | ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| Tithe contributor | Kelly only               | Romeo does not tithe                                   |
| Tithe methodology | 10% of Kelly's net income | Calculated monthly using Kelly's current net income   |
| Allocation timing | Monthly                  | Included as part of the monthly planning process       |
| Override policy   | Permitted                | Must be documented using the standard override process |

### Tithe Methodology

Tithe is calculated as 10% of Kelly's net income received during the planning month.

Example:

If Kelly net income = £2,766.10:

* Tithe = £276.61

Actual amounts should be recalculated during each monthly review using Kelly's current income figures.

### Priority

Tithe should be included as a planned allocation during the monthly planning phase and considered alongside other committed financial obligations.

### Override Rule

Where temporary cashflow pressures exist, any deviation from the standard tithe methodology must be documented as an override within the monthly review.
