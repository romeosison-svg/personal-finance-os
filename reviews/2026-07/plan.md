## Plan Basis

This plan deliberately does **not** start the full bucket system in July.

1. Operate at the **£500 minimum cash buffer** for July.
2. Plan recurring operating spend as if Kelly's **£980.89** June pay uplift did not exist.
3. Cap normalised recurring operating spend at **£1,256.38**.
4. Treat the **£980.89** uplift as non-recurring windfall capacity.
5. Prioritise Amex payoff / rollover reduction before clearing the remaining Barclaycard balance.
6. Hold the **£980.89** uplift as Amex payoff capacity unless a final decision redirects part of it.
7. Defer plumbing, both-Amex pay-in-full, emergency-fund contribution, and Kelly savings replenishment.

This is a constrained plan. It avoids building recurring spending around non-recurring backpay, while using the uplift to reduce the most material near-term card pressure.

July is a transition/control month. The household is not preserving Romeo Amex pay-in-full at all costs this month and is not yet operating a full bucket system. Instead, the priority is to stop the lag where prior-month Amex spending competes with current-month cash. From July onward, Amex may be used as a payment rail only when the spend is intentional, tracked, and backed by available operating cash.
